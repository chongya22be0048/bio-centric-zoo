Research Foundation

Why Arboreal Primates?

Arboreal primates are among the most spatially demanding animals in zoological parks. Their welfare depends not only on habitat size, but on vertical complexity, canopy connectivity and opportunities for natural movement.

FIELD NOTES / 01

Research Justification

Unlike terrestrial animals, arboreal primates use the rainforest in three dimensions. They make an ideal group for investigating how landscape architecture can recreate ecological systems—not simply design enclosures.

Did You Know?

Orangutans share approximately 97% of their DNA with humans. Gibbons can brachiate at up to 55 km/h. Tropical rainforests hold over half of terrestrial biodiversity while covering only about 6% of land.
